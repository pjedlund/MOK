# MOK Design System

## Overview

Also known as "Brand & Style".

MOK is the design system for **mok.nu**, the website of MalmÃ¶
Orienteringsklubb â€” a Swedish orienteering club rooted in Bokskogen, the
beech forest south of MalmÃ¶. The system is built for an event-driven club
site: an upcoming-races feed, past-results recaps, training programmes,
practical info for members, and a quiet log of what the club has been up
to in the woods.

The visual language pairs **MOK ocean blue (#0089BD)** as the brand voice
with **beech-canopy green (#5BA327)** as the secondary accent, set on
**warm cream (#F7F3EC)** surfaces with **charcoal (#1A1919)** text. It
feels grounded rather than corporate â€” closer to a club noticeboard pinned
under tree cover than a SaaS dashboard. Type carries most of the
personality: iA Writer Quattro for both display and body, iA Writer Mono
for code and dense metadata. Depth is conveyed through tonal contrast and
a one-rem top border on every page â€” never through drop shadows.

The system is fluid by construction. Every type and space step is generated
by [Utopia](https://utopia.fyi) and clamped between a 320 px and 1350 px
viewport, so the same tokens describe both phone and desktop without
breakpoint logic. Tokens are authored in `src/_data/designTokens/*.json`,
consumed by Tailwind to produce CSS custom properties, and pushed to
[Penpot](https://design.penpot.app) via `npm run penpot:tokens`. This
DESIGN.md is the third surface â€” for AI agents and humans who want the
system in one readable file.

## Colors

The palette is two saturated brand accents on a warm-neutral foundation.
There is no third brand color â€” supporting tones (rust hover, forest
muted) stay desaturated so they sit beside the two accents without
competing.

### Brand accents

The two saturated voices. Numbered slots (`accent-1`, `accent-2`) rather
than `primary`/`secondary` â€” the numbers are deliberately unloaded so the
slots can be reordered or reweighted in a future revision without
forcing a global rename.

- **`accent-1` â€” MOK Ocean (#0089BD):** The brand voice. Used for the
  logo fill, link underlines, primary buttons, focus outlines, and any
  element that should read as "this is MOK." Strong enough to anchor a
  screen on its own; restrained enough to leave room for typography to
  lead.
- **`accent-2` â€” Beech Canopy (#5BA327):** The forest accent. Used for
  blockquote rules, result pills, eyebrow labels, status indicators, and
  hover transitions away from blue. Borrows its hue from the beech canopy
  in Bokskogen â€” at noon in spring, before the leaves darken.

### Foundation neutrals

The non-saturated anchors. These are not brand voices â€” they're the
paper and ink the brand sits on, and they live in the `surface`,
`on-surface`, `border-top`, and `base-*` token families rather than as
numbered accent slots.

- **Charcoal (#1A1919):** Body text. Sits on warm cream at well above
  WCAG AA contrast and reads as ink rather than soft black. Exposed as
  `on-surface` and `base-darkest`.
- **Warm Cream (#F7F3EC):** Default surface. A warm off-white that
  softens the charcoal and reads as paper. Exposed as `surface` and
  `base-lightest`.
- **Warm Tan (#ddd5c4):** The 1 rem top border that frames every page.
  Exposed as `border-top` and `base-light`.

### Dark theme

The dark theme â€” _natt i Bokskogen_ â€” mirrors the footer of the reference
mockup rather than just inverting the light scale. The background drops to
**charcoal #1A1919** (the same charcoal used as text in light mode), text
rises to **pale beech #DCE8D8**, muted labels shift to **forest moss
#6A8465**, and links lift to **light ocean #71C8E6**. Lifted surfaces and
the body's top border use **deep forest #2a3028** â€” the dark band you'd
see between trees at dusk. The result feels like the same forest at night,
not a colorless inverse of the day theme.

The dark-mode tokens use the `-dark` suffix in the YAML front matter and
map onto the `theme/dark` set in the Penpot DTCG export.

### Design Tokens

See the `colors` block in the front matter. The brand-accent slots
(`accent-1`, `accent-2`) and the foundation tokens (`surface`,
`on-surface`, `border-top`, `headline`, `base-*`) map onto the CSS
custom properties in `src/assets/css/global/base/variables.css`. The
full warm-neutral and canopy ramps (`gray-100` â€¦ `gray-900`,
`orange-100` â€¦ `orange-900`) are exposed for callers that need the
underlying steps.

The legacy `gray` / `orange` palette key names are retained from the
previous design system to avoid a CSS-wide rename â€” the values they
hold are now warm-neutral and forest-canopy respectively. A future
cleanup may rename them; the public front-matter accent keys
(`accent-canopy`, `accent-blue`, `accent-blue-light`,
`accent-green-pale`, `accent-green-deep`) already tell the truth.

The previous design system used `primary` / `secondary` / `tertiary` /
`neutral` slot names; these were dropped because their meaning drifts
between teams and tools (the JEDEE-era article on token naming
conventions records the reasoning). Numbered `accent-N` slots are
deliberately unloaded â€” they describe brand position without claiming a
fixed semantic priority.

## Typography

Type is the personality. Two iA Writer families do all the work:

- **Display & Body â€” iA Writer Quattro:** A single quasi-mono humanist
  serif used for both `h1`â€“`h3` and paragraph copy. Headings use the bold
  cut at tight `leading-fine`/`leading-extrafine`; body uses the regular
  cut at `leading-standard` (1.4) with `letterSpacing: -0.04ch`. Quattro's
  even rhythm and small ink trap detailing carry over from the print
  origins of orienteering maps.
- **Mono â€” iA Writer Mono:** Used for code blocks, inline code, eyebrow
  labels, and dense metadata (date stamps, race distances, course
  designations). Set at the smallest fluid step.

The size scale is fluid (Utopia) with nine steps from `step-min-2`
(13 â†’ 16 px) to `step-6` (56 â†’ 107 px). The same composite tokens drive
every breakpoint â€” no separate mobile/desktop type ramps.

> **Note:** the iA Writer Quattro and Mono `.woff2` files have not yet
> landed in `src/assets/fonts/`. Until they do, the live site falls back
> to the metric-compatible Georgia/Menlo system stack declared in
> `fonts.json`. Adding the woff2 assets and the `@font-face` declarations
> is tracked as a follow-up task.

### Design Tokens

The `typography` front-matter block lists the named composite styles â€”

`label-caps`, `code`. These mirror `src/_data/designTokens/typography.json`

## Layout

The layout follows a **single-column, max-width** model: a 1360 px wrapper
(`--wrapper-width: 85rem`) with fluid gutters that shrink from 31 px down
to 14 px on phones. There is no rigid column grid â€” content flows in a
single editorial column, with cards (event rows, past-result recaps,
category tiles) pulled out via CUBE CSS compositions (Stack, Cluster,
Switcher, Grid).

Spacing uses the Utopia fluid scale, with both fixed steps (`xs`, `s`,
`m`, `l`, `xl`, `2xl`, `3xl`) and **paired steps** (`s-m`, `m-l`,
`l-xl`, â€¦). Paired steps interpolate across viewports more aggressively
than fixed ones â€” they're the right choice for vertical rhythm between
sections, where small phones want compactness and desktops want breathing
room. Fixed steps are used for component-internal padding where rhythm
should stay constant.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth is conveyed through **tonal layers and contrast**, never shadows.
The system is intentionally flat:

- The page background is **warm cream** (`surface`), and elevated
  surfaces (cards, code blocks, lifted nav in dark mode) lift to pure
  white (`surface-accent`) in light, or to **deep forest #2a3028** in
  dark.
- The body has a **persistent 1 rem top border** in `border-top` â€” a
  warm-tan band in light mode (`base-light` #ddd5c4), a deep-forest band
  in dark (`green-deep` #2a3028). This is the single piece of "chrome"
  that survives across all pages, and it doubles as the day/night signal:
  warm tan in dag, deep forest in natt.
- Interactive elements signal state through **color and underline weight**,
  not lift. Hover and focus never use box-shadow.
- The `:focus-visible` ring is a 3 px solid `currentColor` outline,
  offset by 0.3 ch (0.08 em on Firefox).

## Shapes

Corner radii are minimal. Most surfaces are square; a few utility shapes
soften.

- `rounded.sm` (0.1875 rem â‰ˆ 3 px) â€” code blocks, inline code, small chips
- `rounded.md` (0.3 rem â‰ˆ 5 px) â€” buttons, form inputs (when used)
- `rounded.full` (9999 px) â€” pill-shaped tags and the avatar
- The body, articles, headings, hr, and most layout primitives use **no
  corner radius** at all

The visual emphasis is on the warm border-top band, the MOK ocean
underlines, and the rhythm of type â€” not on rounded containers.

## Components

### Body

The page itself. Sets the default font, color, and background; renders
the top accent border that frames every screen.

| Property | Light (dag) | Dark (natt) |
|---|---|---|
| `backgroundColor` | `colors.surface` (cream) | `colors.surface-dark` (charcoal) |
| `textColor` | `colors.on-surface` (charcoal) | `colors.on-surface-dark` (pale beech) |
| `borderTop` | 1 rem `colors.border-top` (warm tan) | 1 rem `colors.border-top-dark` (deep forest) |
| `accentColor` | `colors.accent-blue` (MOK ocean) | `colors.accent-blue-light` (light ocean) |

### Headings (h1â€“h3)

Set in iA Writer Quattro at 700, with tight letter-spacing. `h1` uses
`heading-1` (largest, tightest line-height); `h2`/`h3` step down through
the size scale but keep `leading-fine` (1.2). Color is `headline`, which
matches body color in light mode and lifts to `green-pale` in dark for
visual hierarchy against the charcoal background.

### Links

Inline anchors are styled at 0.2 ex underline thickness, offset 0.3 ch
from the baseline. Underlines are colored with `accent-blue` (MOK ocean)
in light mode and `accent-blue-light` in dark. On hover the underline
shifts to `accent-canopy` (beech canopy green) and the text colors to the
same canopy. Underlines transition over 200 ms.

| State | textColor | underlineColor |
|---|---|---|
| Default | `colors.on-surface` | `colors.accent-blue` |
| Hover | `colors.accent-canopy` | `colors.accent-canopy` |
| Focus-visible | inherited | + 3 px outline at `currentColor` |

### Blockquote

A pulled, indented quote with a heavy left rule.

- Padding: `spacing.m-l` (fluid 14 â†’ 31 px)
- Border-inline-start: 0.5 rem solid `colors.accent-canopy`
- Type: `typography.blockquote` (44 px max), with the last child rendered
  as `typography.citation` (35 px max) for attribution
- Stack rhythm: paragraph children separated by `spacing.m-l`

The canopy-green left rule echoes the past-quote treatment in the MOK
mockup â€” the same green that pulls "result pill" badges and section
eyebrows.

### Inputs

Text inputs and textareas inherit body type. The caret is colored
`accent-canopy`. Surface and corners follow `surface-accent` and
`rounded.md` when explicit container styling is applied.

### Horizontal rule

A 10%-width, 1 px bar in `surface-accent-2`. Acts as a quiet section
divider rather than a hard cut. Margin-block follows
`--flow-space, spacing.m-l`.

### Figure caption

Rendered in `typography.caption` (italic, 22 px max), centered, with a
small bottom padding so the caption sits under the figure rather than
the next paragraph. Color follows `on-surface-accent`.

### Selection

User text selection inverts the document: background becomes the body's
text color, foreground becomes the page background. This produces a
strong visual hit without introducing a new color.

### Focus ring

A 3 px solid `currentColor` outline, 0.3 ch offset. Reduced to 0.08 em on
Firefox to match its rendering. Never replaced by a shadow.

## Do's and Don'ts

- **Do** keep MOK Ocean Blue as the single brand voice on any screen.
  Reach for Beech Canopy Green only as a category cue or a hover
  transition. One brand color is more legible than two.
- **Do** prefer paired spacing tokens (`s-m`, `m-l`) for vertical rhythm
  between sections; reserve fixed steps for inside-component padding.
- **Do** let type carry hierarchy â€” change size and weight before
  changing color.
- **Do** maintain WCAG AA contrast on body text (4.5:1) â€” charcoal on
  warm cream clears AA in light, and pale beech on charcoal clears AAA
  in dark.
- **Do** treat the warm-tan / deep-forest top border as the day/night
  signal. It's the only piece of chrome that survives across pages.
- **Don't** introduce shadows. The system is intentionally flat;
  elevation is tonal.
- **Don't** reach for `accent-red`, `accent-blue-subdued`, or any third
  saturated color as a CTA. They are quiet secondary cues only.
- **Don't** mix corner radii on the same surface. A code block uses
  `rounded.sm`; the page itself uses none. Don't add `rounded.md` to
  inline code.
- **Don't** introduce new font families. iA Writer Quattro and iA Writer
  Mono cover display, body, and code respectively.
- **Don't** hard-code hex values in CSS. Reach for the CSS custom
  properties generated from the JSON tokens (or the matching Penpot
  variables) so a single source of truth flows everywhere.
